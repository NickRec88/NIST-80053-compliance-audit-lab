# NIST-80053-compliance-audit-lab
This project is a simulated compliance assessment based on NIST SP 800-53 Revision 5 and was created to demonstrate the responsibilities of a Junior Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) Analyst.

The assessment was performed against a fictional organization, BrightWave Healthcare Solutions, a healthcare Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) company with approximately 120 employees.

The goal of this project was to simulate a real compliance engagement by interviewing a business stakeholder, documenting evidence, assessing compliance against selected NIST controls, identifying organizational risks, and presenting the assessment results to management.

Company Background

Company: BrightWave Healthcare Solutions

Industry: Healthcare Software (SaaS)

Employees: Approximately 120

Audit Scope: Access Control (AC) Family

Project Objectives

The objectives of this project were to:

Conduct a stakeholder interview to gather information for a compliance assessment.
Perform a practical compliance audit using selected NIST SP 800-53 Access Control (AC) controls.
Document interview responses and supporting evidence in a compliance assessment worksheet.
Evaluate each control and determine its compliance status.
Perform a risk assessment for identified control deficiencies.
Develop recommendations to reduce organizational risk.
Present the audit results in a professional summary to management.
Scope of Assessment

The assessment focused on the following base Access Control (AC) controls:

AC-1 – Access Control Policy and Procedures
AC-2 – Account Management
AC-3 – Access Enforcement
AC-4 – Information Flow Enforcement
AC-5 – Separation of Duties
AC-6 – Least Privilege
AC-7 – Unsuccessful Logon Attempts

Only the base controls were assessed.

Control enhancements (for example, AC-2(1), AC-6(9), etc.) were intentionally excluded from the project to keep the assessment focused on a realistic entry-level compliance engagement. In a real organization, control enhancements would only be assessed if they were included within the organization's selected control baseline or otherwise required.

Audit Methodology

The assessment followed a simplified compliance audit workflow similar to what a Junior GRC Analyst may perform.

The process included:

Interviewing the Operations Manager.
Collecting information regarding organizational processes.
Identifying evidence that would normally be reviewed during an assessment.
Assessing each selected NIST SP 800-53 control.
Documenting findings and recommendations.
Performing a risk assessment for supported observations.
Presenting the assessment results to management.

Where supporting evidence was referenced, the project assumed the documentation existed because the organization is fictional.

These documents represent the types of evidence that would typically be requested during a compliance assessment.

Risk Assessment

Following completion of the compliance assessment, a risk register was developed for findings that were supported by the evidence collected during the interview.

Not every finding resulted in a risk entry.

Where the Operations Manager did not own a technical control or could not provide sufficient information, those items were documented as requiring additional validation from the IT Manager or Information Security team rather than being treated as confirmed risks.

This approach reflects the importance of making evidence-based conclusions during an audit.

Use of Artificial Intelligence

Artificial Intelligence (AI) was used as a learning and simulation tool throughout this project.

Specifically, AI was used to:

Simulate the role of the Operations Manager during the stakeholder interview.
Provide realistic responses to audit questions within a fictional business environment.
Review interview questions and provide feedback on how they could be improved.
Challenge audit conclusions and encourage evidence-based reasoning.
Review completed audit documentation and risk assessments for clarity and professionalism.

The audit decisions, compliance determinations, risk acceptance, and final documentation were completed by the project author. AI was used as a collaborative learning tool and simulated interview participant rather than as a replacement for the auditor's judgment.

Key Takeaways

This project reinforced the importance of making audit conclusions based on evidence rather than assumptions. It also demonstrated how different stakeholders contribute different pieces of information during an assessment and why additional interviews with technical control owners may be required before reaching conclusions on technical controls.

The project reflects a realistic, entry-level GRC workflow focused on planning, interviewing, documenting evidence, assessing compliance, evaluating risk, and communicating results to management.
